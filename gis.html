GIS stands for Geographic Information System. It is a system that is designed to capture, store, manipulate, analyze, and present geographical data. GIS allows users to view, understand, and interpret spatial data in the form of maps and charts. GIS technology is used in a variety of applications such as urban planning, environmental monitoring, natural resource management, public health, and transportation. GIS software can be used to create maps, analyze patterns and relationships, and make predictions based on spatial data. GIS is based on the concept of geospatial analysis, which is the process of analyzing and interpreting data that is related to a specific location on the Earthâ€™s surface. This includes data on the physical features of the Earth, such as landforms, water bodies, and vegetation, as well as data on human activities, such as population density, transportation networks, and economic activity.
